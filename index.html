Full Story

I was born on July 9, 2001 in Coimbra, Portugal. Since I was 7 years old, I always told my parents, "When I grow up, I want to be a computer engineer." Obviously, I had the typical childhood desires of being a firefighter, police officer, football player, and so on. But from an early age, my goals began to take shape, and it's a good quality of mine to stay firm in my principles and desires. The years went by, and my desire to program kept growing until I had my first experience with software to develop a small Mario-style game. Years later, I had my first experience with pure programming, where a colleague and I developed a purely visual operating system that closely resembled the Windows of that time. In 2019, my first challenge began when I started my bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering at the University of Coimbra, where I learned a lot, and after 3 years, I finally obtained the coveted title of Computer Engineer. But I didn't stop there. Since I was a child, I always enjoyed thinking outside the box, taking toys and using them in unconventional ways. This often led to losing my toys, but it was a thought process outside the norm that I now recognize as the reason for my great interest in computer security. Security requires a lot of creative and out-of-the-box thinking, so I decided to take on another challenge, which is pursuing a master's degree in Information Security, also at the University of Coimbra. And here I am, ...
